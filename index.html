Explore Data Project

Why are outdoor playgrounds important for kids development?

Every kid needs outdoor playtime for a daily refresher.Even in the cold winter season of East Boston, 66.7% of parents still bring their kids to playgrounds no less than3-5 times per month. Lots of parents underestimate the importance of outdoor activities to enhance imagination and open-ended play. Indoor play toys, for example, LEGO, let kids using blocks to construct specific objects, with step-by-step guidelines. But if kids always need directions, they start to lose their creativity. This is part of the reason why indoor toys cannot replace the importance of outdoor playgrounds. In the interview with Aldo Ghirin, Senior Planner in Boston Parks & Recreation Department, he mentioned that the city of Boston also is trying to build more playgrounds in the edge of the larger parks to shorter the travel distance for parents and their young kids.
East Boston has a higher percent of children population compared to Boston. In order to understand what facilities in playgrounds are available for those large children groups, I posted a survey on Facebook and Lean in Boston to learn more about the needs of parents.
